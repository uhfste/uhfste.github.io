Potential causes of pica in sheep:

- Nutrient deficiencies - Sheep lacking certain minerals such as phosphorus, sodium, copper, and iron may start consuming non-food items trying to obtain these nutrients.

- Boredom and stress - Sheep without enough mental stimulation or under anxiety may start chewing on or eating odd things. Providing enrichment with toys, brushes, and human interaction could help with boredom and stress.

- Parasites - Worms, liver flukes, coccidia and other parasites can irritate the gut causing discomfort leading sheep to eat unusual items to self-medicate. Regular deworming and clean housing helps prevent parasites.

- Toxic exposure - Ingesting contaminants like lead, arsenic, mercury from feed, bedding or soil could contribute to pica. Testing surroundings for toxins is advisable.

- Diet imbalance - Too much of certain minerals such as sodium and calcium in the diet may prompt pica behavior. Evaluating mineral levels in feed is recommended.

- Oral injury - Mouth or tooth damage can cause pain and potentially lead to pica. Regular dental checkups and care may help.

- Disease - Illnesses like rabies, scrapie and neurological conditions could be linked to pica starting suddenly. Veterinary assessment is recommended if this occurs.

- Genetic tendency - Some sheep may be prone to pica inherently. Removing them from breeding stock could decrease this.
