Treatments for mastitis in ewes (ovines):

- Antibiotics: Antibiotics are usually the first line of treatment for mastitis in ewes. Common antibiotics used include penicillin, ceftiofur, ampicillin, amoxicillin/clavulanic acid. Antibiotics are given either by intramammary injection into the infected gland or systemically. The course of treatment typically lasts 5-7 days.

- NSAIDs: Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs like flunixin meglumine may be given to help reduce swelling and pain associated with mastitis.

- Dry off/strip milk: The infected gland should be stripped of milk completely to help drain and dry out the infected tissue. Stripping is often repeated several times a day.

- Hot compresses: Applying warm, moist compresses to the infected mammary gland may help increase blood flow and drainage.

- Iodine: Topical antiseptics like iodine can be applied to the teat and udder to help prevent spread of infection.

- Culling: In cases of recurring or chronic mastitis that does not respond to treatment, culling the ewe may be necessary to prevent spread to others in the flock.

- Supportive care: Ensuring proper nutrition and potentially fluids if the ewe shows systemic illness signs from mastitis. Removing the lamb from nursing may also help the ewe recover.

The key is early detection and prompt treatment with appropriate antibiotics under veterinary guidance for best outcomes with mastitis in sheep. Recurrence risks may be prevented through hygiene practices.
