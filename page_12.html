Johnes disease in sheep and goats:

Causes
- Caused by Mycobacterium avium subspecies paratuberculosis (MAP) bacteria
- Spread through feces of infected animals
- Ingested through contaminated milk, colostrum, feed, or environment

Symptoms
- Usually appear 2+ years after infection
- Weight loss and diarrhea despite normal appetite
- Decreased milk production in dairy animals
- Bottle jaw (swelling under jaw) due to protein loss

Other Features
- No cure, antibiotics only help control bacterial shedding
- Blood tests, fecal culture, and biopsies used for diagnosis
- Control through testing, culling infected animals, and good sanitation
- Vaccines being researched but none completely effective yet
- Can cause major economic losses if not well managed

In summary, Johnes is a chronic intestinal infection of sheep and goats caused by MAP bacteria, leading to wasting and diarrhea years after initial infection. Careful control measures are needed to limit spread and impact on herds.
