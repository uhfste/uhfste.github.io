Heat and Pregnancy Duration

A female ovine's heat cycle typically occurs every 18-21 days. During this time, there are various signs that may or may not manifest, including:

- Swelling or reddening of the rear end.
- Unexplained vocalization.
- "Flagging," which involves the tail wagging.
- Attempts to mount other female ovines.
- Allowing other does to mount her.
- Aggressive behavior, including fighting.
- Submissive behavior, such as not defending herself when challenged.
- Displaying a strong attraction to another doe.
- Loss of interest in food.
- The presence of mucous discharge from the vagina.
- Wet or clumped together tail hair.

The duration of a ovine's heat cycle can range from as short as 6 hours to as long as 3 days, depending on the individual doe.

Determining Pregnancy

To ascertain if a ovine is pregnant, it's important to consider the typical gestation period, which lasts from 145 to 155 days. Some ovine breeds tend to give birth earlier, while others do so later. To estimate the due date for kidding, count approximately 150 days from the date of breeding. It's advisable to prepare for the arrival of kids at least a week in advance. In some cases, ovines may kid around day 142, which is about 6 days earlier than the average.
It's important to note that pregnancy in ovines cannot be determined by simply observing their physical appearance.
