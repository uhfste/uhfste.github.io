Main causes of emaciation (thinness and weight loss) in sheep and goats:

1. Parasitic infections - Stomach and intestinal parasites like worms and protozoa can lead to diarrhea, malnutrition, and appetite loss resulting in emaciation over time.

2. Infectious diseases - Bacterial and viral illnesses like Johne's disease, caseous lymphadenitis, footrot can reduce feed intake due to fever and discomfort leading to weight loss.

3. Dental disorders - Abnormal tooth wear, dental abscesses and tooth loss can make eating very painful and difficult leading to inadequate nutrition.

4. Nutritional deficiencies - Insufficient quality and quantity of roughage, concentrates, proteins, vitamins and minerals in the diet fails to meet nutritional requirements resulting in emaciation.

5. Internal parasites - Flukes, lungworms and other parasites in organs can impair nutrient absorption leading to muscle wasting.

6. Cancer - Lymphosarcoma, reticulum cell sarcoma and other cancers can cause reduced appetite, metabolic issues leading to emaciation.

7. Chronic diseases - Illnesses like ovine pulmonary adenocarcinoma, Maedi visna, caseous lymphadenitis resulting in progressive emaciation.

8. Stress - Heat, cold, overcrowding, transport stress can depress appetite and growth rate leading to weight loss.

9. Endocrine disorders - Diseases of thyroid, adrenal glands can cause hormonal imbalances, metabolic issues leading to emaciation.
