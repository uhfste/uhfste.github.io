Uterine prolapse in ovines

Commonly referred to as "ewe prolapse," is a condition in which the uterus of a female sheep protrudes from the vaginal opening. It can occur during or after lambing, and it is a serious condition that requires prompt attention and treatment. Uterine prolapse is more common in older ewes or those with multiple pregnancies.

**Causes of Uterine Prolapse in Ovines:**
Several factors can contribute to uterine prolapse in sheep:

1. **Weakness of uterine ligaments:** This can be due to genetic factors or nutritional deficiencies.
2. **Difficult or prolonged labor:** If the lambing process is difficult or takes a long time, it can put excessive strain on the uterus.
3. **Large litter size:** Ewes carrying multiple lambs are at a higher risk of uterine prolapse.
4. **Poor body condition:** Malnutrition or poor overall health can weaken the supporting structures of the uterus.

**Treatment of Uterine Prolapse in Ovines:**
Uterine prolapse is an emergency that requires immediate attention. Here are the steps involved in treating uterine prolapse in sheep:

1. **Isolate and restrain the ewe:** Move the sheep to a clean, dry area and gently restrain her to prevent further injury.

2. **Clean the prolapsed uterus:** Gently clean the exposed uterus and surrounding area with warm, clean water and a mild antiseptic solution to reduce the risk of infection.

3. **Reduce the prolapse:** Push the uterus back into the ewe's vagina carefully. Lubrication may be used to facilitate this process. It is crucial to be gentle to avoid causing further damage.

4. **Use a retention device:** To keep the uterus in place and prevent it from prolapsing again, a retention device such as a uterine harness or a modified spoon-shaped instrument called a "uterosacral suture" can be used. This should be done by a veterinarian or an experienced individual.

5. **Administer antibiotics:** To prevent or treat any potential infections, administer appropriate antibiotics as prescribed by a veterinarian.

6. **Provide supportive care:** Keep the ewe in a clean and dry environment, and provide her with food, water, and close monitoring. It's essential to make sure she is comfortable during the recovery period.

7. **Consult a veterinarian:** Even if you manage to successfully reduce the prolapse, it is advisable to consult a veterinarian for a thorough examination and further guidance on post-treatment care.

Preventing uterine prolapse in ewes involves maintaining their overall health, providing proper nutrition, and carefully managing the lambing process. If you notice any signs of uterine prolapse in your sheep, seek immediate veterinary assistance, as untreated cases can lead to serious complications and even death.
