Antibiotics

It is essential to complete the entire antibiotic course. Administer antibiotics for a minimum of five days, even if the symptoms resolve. Prematurely discontinuing antibiotics often leads to a relapse or the emergence of antibiotic-resistant bacteria. Continue treatment for 30 hours after the animal no longer exhibits symptoms.

Note on Antibiotics: It should not be viewed as a universal remedy for all medical issues. Using antibiotics as a first response to any apparent health problem can contribute to antibiotic resistance. Therefore, it is crucial to reserve their use for treating infections as intended. Additionally, it's important to be aware that antibiotics can place significant stress on the immune system and have adverse effects on the digestive system.

1-Drug Name: Ceftiofur sodium - 1mg/ml

Brand Names: Naxel

This is a broad-spectrum antibiotic primarily utilized for respiratory ailments such as pneumonia in ovines.

Ovine dosage: Administer via SQ Injection at a rate of 1 ml per 50 lbs. twice daily.

Milk withholding time: None

Notes:

Naxel is often used in conjunction with penicillin.
Naxel is relatively expensive and has a short shelf life of one week once the powder is reconstituted. It can be stored in the freezer for up to 8 weeks once reconstituted.
Its effectiveness may diminish after the initial days of infection, and another antibiotic may be recommended.

2-Drug Name: Ceftiofur hydrochloride

Brand Names: Excenel

This is a second-generation penicillin, categorized as a broad-spectrum antibiotic used primarily for respiratory issues and control of major pathogens associated with foot rot in ovines.

Ovine dosage: Administer via SQ Injection at a rate of 1 ml per 25 lbs. once daily.

Milk withholding time: None

3-Drug Name: Oxytetracycline - 200 mg/ml

Brand Names: LA-200, Oxy-Tet 200, Bio-Mycin 200, Liquamycin LA 200, Geomycin 200, Agrimycin 200, Maxim 200

"أحسن الأنواع مشهور في السعودية تحت اسم تيراميسين"

This medication is preferred for addressing common causes of contagious keratoconjunctivitis ("pinkeye"), mycoplasma, chlamydia, metritis following freshening, and navel or joint ill in young kids. It is also effective against most mastitis-causing bacteria via systemic injection.

ovine dosage: Administer via SQ Injection at a rate of 4.5 ml per 100 lbs. every 36-48 hours (3 shots) or 3 ml per 100 lbs. once daily. For pink eye, apply a few drops of oxytetracycline directly into the eye in addition to injections(أو ممكن تقطر بنسيلين بس يكون مائي).

Milk withholding time: 12-18 days

Notes:

Do not confuse this drug with Tylan 200; they are not the same medication.
Oxytetracycline is counteracted by milk, so do not use it in nursing animals or those that have consumed milk.
As it ages, tetracycline may become dark and somewhat painful upon injection, but it remains effective.
Avoid using any tetracycline on pregnant does or kids under six months old, as it may interfere with bone and teeth development.
Do not administer Oxytetracycline and Penicillin simultaneously, as this can reduce the effectiveness of Penicillin.
Resistance to oxytetracycline has been reported in certain bacteria strains.
It is often used in conjunction with sulfonamide drugs like sulfadimethoxine (Albon).

4-Drug Name: Procaine Penicillin G - 300,000 units Procaine Penicillin per ml

Brand Names: Pfi-Pen, Agri-cillin, US Vet Penicillin G Procaine

Penicillin is effective against gram-positive bacteria, making it suitable for streptococcus infections, chronic pneumonia, and other infections in ovines.

Ovine dosage: Administer via SQ Injection at a rate of 1 ml per 15 lbs. twice daily for a minimum of 5 days, or until all symptoms have disappeared, whichever occurs first.

Milk withholding time: 14-20 days

Notes:

Resistance to this drug is widespread.
Avoid combining Oxytetracycline and Penicillin, as this can diminish the effectiveness of Penicillin.

5-Drug Name: Penicillin Procaine & Penicillin Benzathine combo - 150,000 penicillin units G procaine & 150,000 units penicillin benzathine per ml

Brand Names: Twin Pen, Pen BP-48, Crystiben, US Vet Penicillin Benzathine & Penicillin G Procaine

Penicillin, effective against gram-positive bacteria, is suitable for streptococcus infections, chronic pneumonia, and other infections in ovines.

Ovine dosage: Administer via SQ Injection at a rate of 1 ml per 25 lbs. once a day for at least 5 days or until all symptoms have disappeared.

Milk withholding time: 25-30 days

 

Notes:

Resistance is widespread for both penicillin procaine and benzathine, with the latter having a slow absorption rate.
Avoid combining Oxytetracycline and Penicillin, as this can diminish the effectiveness of Penicillin.

6-Drug Name: Tylosin - 200 mg/ml

Brand Names: Tylan 200, Tylosin 200mg Injection

Tylosin is effective against mycoplasma, chlamydia, and rickettsia. It is used for upper respiratory infections, certain forms of enteritis, and mycoplasma arthritis in young kids.

Goat dosage: Administer via SQ Injection at a rate of 1 ml per 20 lbs. once a day for 5 days.

Milk withholding time: 8 days

Notes: Tylan injections can be quite painful, which is why they are typically avoided.

7-Drug Name: Florfenicol - 300mg/ml

Brand Names: Nuflor

Florfenicol has a broad spectrum of activity against both gram-negative and gram-positive bacteria, primarily exhibiting bacteriostatic effects against salmonellae and E. coli.

Ovine dosage: Administer via SQ Injection at a rate of 3 ml per 50 lbs. every 4 days or 3.5 ml per 100 lbs. in the neck area every 2 days.

Milk withholding time: 28 days

Notes:

Florfenicol injections may cause discomfort.
Effectiveness may decrease unless used in the initial days of infection.
