(القرع)treatments for ringworm infection in sheep (ovines):

- Miconazole or bifonazole (antifungal creams/ointments) - These can be applied topically to affected areas 2-3 times per day. Common brands include Daktarin or Lotrimin. Treatment usually lasts 2-4 weeks.

- Clip or shear affected areas - Removing wool makes topical treatments more effective and reduces spreading.
- Lime sulfur dips - Sheep can be dipped in a lime sulfur solution. This treats the entire body and helps prevent spread. Dips should be done weekly for 3-4 weeks.

- Ketoconazole shampoo - This antifungal shampoo can be massaged onto affected areas and left on for several minutes before rinsing off. Use 2-3 times per week.

- Iodine solutions - Applying tincture of iodine or povidone-iodine solutions to lesions helps kill the fungus. Re-apply daily until cleared.

- Antifungal medications - Oral medications like griseofulvin can be given if ringworm has spread extensively. Treatment usually lasts 4-6 weeks.

- Practice good hygiene and disinfection - Clean barns, shearing equipment, fences etc. to limit environmental contamination with the fungus.

- Isolate infected sheep - Separating and quarantining infected animals helps control spread within the flock.
