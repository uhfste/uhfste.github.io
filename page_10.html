Neurological signs in sheep and goats, along with their treatments:

- Listeriosis

Caused by the bacteria Listeria monocytogenes. Symptoms include circling, depression, head tilt, and facial paralysis. Treat with antibiotics like penicillin. Ensure proper sanitation.

- Polioencephalomalacia (PEM)

Caused by thiamine deficiency. Sheep will have blindness, head pressing, star gazing, tremors. Treat by injecting or giving thiamine supplements. Provide adequate nutrition.

- Rabies

Viral disease transmitted by infected animals. Causes aggression, seizures, paralysis. No treatment, but can vaccinate sheep to prevent infection.

- Tetanus

Caused by Clostridium tetani bacterium. Rigidity, lockjaw, stiff gait. Treat with antitoxin and antibiotics. Prevent by vaccinating.

- Scrapie

Fatal, degenerative prion disease. Nervousness, scraping, loss of coordination. No treatment or prevention. Culling recommended.

- Ovine Progressive Pneumonia (OPP)

Caused by a retrovirus. Can cause weakness, lethargy, stumbling. No treatment, but can control via testing and culling.

- Meningeal Worm

Parasitic infection by Parelaphostrongylus tenuis. Ataxia, blindness, circling. Deworm with anthelmintics containing praziquantel. Prevent with pasture management.

- Abscess or injury to brain

Can cause varying neurological symptoms. Treat cause of abscess if possible. Manage recovery.
